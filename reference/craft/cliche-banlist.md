# Cliché Banlist

Versioned reference for `manuscript_checker.py`. Each entry: `- phrase (severity: high|medium)`.
Optional source attribution after ` — `.

Severity guide:
- **high** — Unambiguously stale; flag on first occurrence.
- **medium** — Context-dependent; flag when accumulated or unironic.

## Filter Verbs
<!-- source: craft research (masterclass, bookbutchers) -->
- began to (severity: high) — filters action; use direct verb instead
- started to (severity: high) — same as "began to"
- seemed to (severity: medium) — POV hedge; trust the reader
- appeared to (severity: medium) — epistemic distancing
- was able to (severity: high) — wordy; just use the verb
- tried to (severity: medium) — weakens commitment; commit to the action
- managed to (severity: medium) — implies struggle where none is needed
- had to (severity: medium) — overused obligation framing
- decided to (severity: medium) — tell not show for internal choice
- chose to (severity: medium) — redundant with the action itself
